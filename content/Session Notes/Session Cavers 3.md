- Ryo, Mithe, the Criminal (later discovered to be named [[Artemis]]), and the Dwarf ([[Volk Stormhammer]]) enter a narrow path towards a 7 ft seam in the wall. Going through the wall brings them to a circular room/round chamber of black stone, identified by the Dwarf to be natural [[Meteorite]]. The entire room is composed of this material. 
- Living infused [[Cobalt]] is rare. Encountering natural [[Meteorite]] is unheard of. There is no known source of natural meteorite outside of hodge-podge alchemies.
- In the center of the room is a cylindric table with a concave top.
- Asking the dwarf about the possibility of another world elicits this response, "*The forefathers did spoke, yes they spoke, when you lot were not in this subsystem but elsewhere. I do not know the specifics for even the forefathers said that they vowed to never speak of this topic, and only once we traded with you, when we were alone in the stone.*"
- On the ceiling is a map of yellow dots connected by semi-geometric straight lines that directly match the pattern Mithe had visions of in the pool from [[Session Cavers 2]]. The material of star map looked to have a glassy-fluidy yellow, in contrast to the glassy sheen on ebony darkness. Casting Prestidigitation disappears if you fully recreate ceiling patterns, so magic works perfectly until it resembles anything too close to the star map--almost as if it is a system of rule or law that cannot be broken.
- There are three symbols that look as if part of the wall around the room. Mithe touches them all and experiences overwhelming emotions, concepts, and words.
	- **Green Book.** Luminescent color. Feelings of regality, inevitability, and a sense of loss. "*Fate's Final Rest*".
	- **Blue Scepter.** "*Humanity's Unyielding Spirit*".
	- **Red Crown.** Many emotions dwell overwhelmingly--sadness, melancholy, anger, betrayal--but also a degree of happiness, certainty, and faith. "*The end of the starborn*." "*My sacrifice is your duty*."
- Mithe then feels genuine pain coarse through her (-3 psychic dmg) and hears a harsher stricken voice: 
  "*Friend, I ask you this--not just out of obligation or duty, or even of the great astronomers. I ask this of you because despite our differences, despite the past, you whom has lost so much, who has given everything. Yet I ask for more. Will you undertake this path? Will you pledge yourself--the loss your body, of your potential existence--to be the world's warden, chosen out of callousness.*"
- "*A lord's final people*."
- Ryo taps the concave centerpiece with his obsidian [[Key]], causing light to start from the "sky constellation" on the ceiling, that then falls and reflects onto the 3 images.
- Rotating the cylinder causes the room walls to also rotate, and the constellation dots and lines to shift slightly and realign. Rotating 270 times appears to invert the reflection positioning from how it was originally. Rotating 540 times brings us back to the original pattern. Looking through the slit while rotating uncovers another engraved message carved on the wall behind, "*[[time]] leaves the universe's burdens*", as well as another opening just slightly right of the opening that we came through.

- Going through the second opening reveals a narrow hallway with thousands of specks of splashes on the walls, dots emitting light against a sea of blackness. The dwarf sees that each plinth between the [[glass]] panels of stars has an inscription in an unknown language on it. 5 [[mask]]s, with reeds stuck in their mouths, are placed diagonally.
	- The first mask is made of obsidian. Touching the obsidian [[key]] against it reveals the translation of the wall engravings, "*Before the first veil was woven, the universe was our anchor--and I was its guide*."
	- The third mask reed comes out freely and has a thin charcoal line that says "*For the many masks you've worn throughout time*."
	- The fifth mask reed is shaped like a key. Its inscription says "*For the life you left behind*."
![[Pasted image 20251127133537.png]]
- Players use the fifth mask reed to unlock the door at the end of the hall, which leads them to an exit. The door closes and disappears behind them, and players find themselves at the last floor of the chasm all the way down. Beyond the door were four ancient machines, the first three giving their thumps in beat, while the fourth struggles on the fourth beat. The thumps pound in their skulls. In this area they see some small figures way above them, but no path up is visible.
