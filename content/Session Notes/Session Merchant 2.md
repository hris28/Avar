Aldred's Insights (On the 7th Fleet)

1. Who are the governing bodies of the 7th fleet -> not under the militarum, but under the nautica liticus that controls the general navy. Naval groups generally follow their own authority. This fleet is led by Admiral Galewind and one of its 2 deputies commodore Sal'vin. They directly answer to the capital but its weird bc its associated with the militarum but not under the militarum, but also associated with the nobles since they general command the navy on commision. At the same time they associate with the religious groups bc they have money.
2. What do they do historically -> They are generally used to secure trade and frontiers of the empire, along with disembarking and rapidly redeploying troops. There are naval battles against other empires and creatures that lurk in the bodies of water. Ship travel is faster, but more dangerous nowadays than road bc roads are mostly secured and most monsters are purged, but the same cannot be said for the bodies of water. Not supposed to shoot naval flares and stuff since they draw attention and attract some things that lurk in the water. They theoretically have the resources to take over a small city bc they do have marines. But generally they only interact with cities to re-supply.
3. Why is the ship called the martyr -> The 7th fleet is the fleet with the most storied history. Supposedly they were originally an auxilary relatively minor fleet in historic times that was used to patrol the outskirts. However during the cataclysm when everything started falling apart, they joined the pilgrims to flee underground so they were the only fleet to survive. The martyr is the **flagship of the 7th fleet** bc it was one of the original galleons of a significiantly larger size of the pre-ships. It has access to things that newer ships dont have access to like producing water and weaponry that is trying to be understood but is just following manuals from old. Its called the martyr bc it is one of the surviving fleets from cataclysm-times; it has been in service for over 600 years and is considered the pride of the navy with some new flagships that have been trying to replicate it but to no avail.
