# Velderra: The Last Breath of Heaven, a Preamble

Before the caves. Before the silence. Before the world slipped away like water through desperate fingers.
There was _light_.
__ __
The world still has a sky.

It is not quiet. Towers of brass, stone, and glass turn their faces upward, reading the slow grammar of the constellations. Councils meet under domes of moving light, choosing harvests, alliances, and voyages by the drift of stars. Priests learn their calendars from comets; judges keep hours by the faint pulse of distant suns.

This is [[Velderra]], one of the major territories of the surface world, where civilization moves to celestial rhythm. The heavens do not lie, and so neither do those who read them truly.

Some are born with the night in their [[blood]]: [[Stellarborn]], marked by a talent that does not come from tutors or kings. A very few climb further and do not come back the same. People whisper of them as [[Celestial]]s, though no one agrees on what price they paid to wear that name.

You believed yourselves enlightened. You were enlightened. In observatories and academies, you mapped the spaces between worlds. You reached for understanding with hands that trembled not from fear, but from wonder.

And that reaching changed everything.
__ __
Tonight the language of heaven goes wrong.

Lines that should be steady smear and slip. A soundless wind moves through observatories and waystations at once, and instruments that have never faltered begin to lie. Messages arrive half-remembered. Sailors see two horizons. Children wake with someone else's dream in their mouths.

The warning was sent days ago, then hours ago, then not at all: each time true until you try to hold it.

The scholars argue about causes while a simpler [[truth]] crawls through the city like a draft: something from beyond has learned how to live inside what we are.

They have a name for it in old field notes that were meant to be burned: **[[Urd]]**.

Not spiders, not exactly. More like a hunger that learned to build webs, stepping through [[seam]]s between worlds and leaving forgetting behind them. They came through a tear you did not mean to make: scholars seeking new worlds, new knowledge, new power, reaching through the [[veil]] until something reached back.

You fought them with everything you had. Velderra's greatest warriors, her brightest Stellarborn, her most devoted astronomers calculating strike patterns by planetary motion, all thrown against the incursion.

And in fighting them, you learned the terrible truth.
The Urd were not the invasion. They were the **symptom**, revealers of another truth.
__ __
Because when your scholars dissected their magic, when your astronomers mapped the dimensional [[fracture]]s, when your [[Stellarborn]] peered too deeply into the spaces between stars, you found it.

Or rather, you remembered that you had _forgotten_ it.

Something ancient. Something woven so deeply into the foundation of humanity that you had never thought to look. A presence in the bedrock of your souls, patient as stone, vast as the sky you so loved to chart. It had always been there, sleeping in the spaces between thought and memory, between what you knew and what you could never quite recall.

The [[Urd]] nested where no sentry stands: in prayers, in lullabies, in the part of a leader that wants to be certain. But they are merely the vector. What truly matters lurks beneath: a [[wound]] in the core of humanity itself, something that had slumbered since before the first tower rose.

**We were told to warn the world. We did. Then we forgot we had.**

The astronomers saw it first in calculations that suddenly refused to align. The stars were right, but the answer was wrong. Prophets woke gasping from dreams of a [[wound]] with no edges, a hunger wearing familiar faces. Some tried to warn the councils. Their words tangled on tongues that had spoken clearly yesterday.

The world was forgetting to save itself.
And then the sky began to break.

Not invasion. Not war. Not conquest.

_**Consequence.**_

Whatever slept beneath had been disturbed, and reality itself was rejecting the infection. The heavens you had trusted to guide you now turned their faces away. Magic, beautiful and terrible, the gift that made you Stellarborn and raised your Celestials, began to burn at the touch.

The cataclysm does not begin with fire. It begins with a breath the world forgets to take.
__ __
You stand on Velderra in the hour before the maps go useless.

You are watchmen, couriers, keepers of lamps, star-readers, and fighters who learned to move without orders when the sky grows loud. Around you, civilization crumbles not with a roar but with a whisper: people forgetting why they're running, forgetting the names of those they're trying to save, forgetting that there was ever anything to remember.

Some have already fled. They call it survival. Perhaps it is.
But you? **You remain.**

Not because you believe you can stop what's coming. The stars have already shown you that calculus. You remain because there are three things that must be preserved, three anchors that might hold the world together when memory itself begins to fray.

A [[prophet]] waits on the high stair, a man who has seen many tomorrows and only one for himself. He knows the end of his road and has one last errand to press into living hands. In his possession is a ledger, heavy and bound in green leather that seems to hold its own light. He cannot carry it where it needs to go. Not anymore.

You will be asked to bear this weight across a city that is turning to [[glass]] and wind, to choose which beacon to save when there are not enough hands, to decide what story should outlive your bodies when the counting starts. You will hold back what can be held, and when that fails you will buy time the only way time is bought.
__ __
The prophet will look each of you in the eyes and speak plainly:

> "You are not abandoning anyone. You are spending yourselves. Generations… comrades… the future… yes. I have looked up and I have seen the bill come due. There is only one choice that secures tomorrow, and it costs everything tonight. Take this where the rock runs deep. Deliver it to those who will forget why they needed it. If it reaches them, there is **hope**. [[Fate]] may have disappeared but hope remains."

He will hand you the ledger, and you will feel its weight settle into your bones. Inside are names. Streets. [[Truth]]s that the coming darkness will try to erase. A list of worlds...dreams...places...of humanity. A story of what must not be forgotten, even when forgetting becomes the world's only language.

Years from now, someone will find it. It will light the world ablaze again, because some truths are too large to carry. But it will also guide them, because you carried it first. We may only hope they are better prepared.
__ __
**You will not survive this night. That is not the point.**

The point is that someone will. And when they do, when they stumble through cave systems anums hence, when they find a [[meteorite]] chamber with a star map on the ceiling, when they touch symbols that pulse with emotions they don't understand, they will be touching the [[echo]]es of what you carried.

They will look up at a sky they've never seen and feel something stir in their chest: not memory, but the shape of memory. They will not know your names. They will not remember your faces.

But they will know, in the way a child knows safety before it learns the word, that someone loved the world enough to let it cost them everything.

> This is not a story of heroes who saved everything.
> This is an elegy for those who saved **anything** at all.
> And it is beautiful, and it is terrible, and it is _necessary_.

**The Last Breath of Heaven is upon you.
Walk. Carry what must be carried. The next step is the one that matters.**
