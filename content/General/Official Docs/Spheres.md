# Spheres

{The air thickens. Not with sound, but with the absence of sound where sound should be. Your breath catches mid-inhale as if reality itself hesitates. As you exhale you notice a frosty breath permeate} 

To the [[Watcher of the Threaded Paths]], 

Your recent movements have not gone unnoticed. The Council grows... concerned is perhaps too strong a word. Curious, then. Curious about the nature of your interventions in the matters of the Prisoned World. 

We understand the compulsion. The [[Everfrost]] advances on our own shores as well. The old [[seal]]s strain. What sleeps beneath their stone remembers waking, and the [[hymn]] that bound it grows thin with each generation that forgets why they sing. You see the pattern repeating across [[spheres]], the recursion of their reaching, and you move to intervene as you have before, in other places, during other times. 

But the Covenant is clear: observation, not orchestration. The Prisoned bear their sentence precisely because they could not be trusted with unbounded agency. To guide them too directly is to undermine the very purpose of their isolation. They must choose their own iterations, even if those choices lead to repetition of ancient errors. 

Your messenger walks their passages now, whispering [[truth]]s to those who carry burdens they do not yet understand. The warrior received his warning. The carriers moved as you foresaw. The foundation holds because you ensured the right hands placed the right weights at the right thresholds. 

Is this observation? Or have you become architect once more? 

I do not write to condemn. The distances between our stations are too vast for such simplicities, and this message itself risks much in its traversal. The great Auric channels are not secure at such remove, and there are [[eye]]s that watch for signatures of interference across the planar. I must be brief and oblique lest this relay be traced.

Consider only this: if their world must break again to learn, perhaps that breaking is the lesson. If their reaching awakens what lies coiled beneath them, perhaps they must face what they disturbed rather than be shielded from consequence. 

You were once their kind. You walked surface and stone. You made their choices and paid their prices. Perhaps this is why you cannot resist the pull to guide them, even as our own sphere grows colder and the settlements retreat further from the advancing ice. 

But remember: the [[Threaded Paths]] are not meant to be walked by those who already know the destination. The purpose of the labyrinth is the *{interference makes the writing incomprehensible}*. 

Their ice will claim what it claims. Their [[seal]]s will hold or break by hands that must choose freely. The [[wound]] beneath their world is theirs to understand, not yours to explain. We face our own encroaching cold. Perhaps you should attend to nearer concerns. 

Step back, old friend. Watch, as we were meant to watch. Or do not. But know that continued intervention may draw attention you have thus far avoided. There are those on the Council who remember your first departure from protocol. Who remember the price you paid to maintain your station despite it. They will not permit a second deviation. 

This is the only warning I can offer across such vast reticulation. 

The Everfrost advances faster each season here. The outer settlements have already been abandoned. But I believe there may be a way forward, I am starting my own investigations now. I wish I could bring you better news. 

May the Aethrium grant you clarity in these darkening days. 

Signed, 
[[The Witness]]
