

> _The most important step a mind can take is a question. Answers end roads; a question lays the next stone._


> _The young man listened, his ash-stained cloak still smoking from the battle above._ 
> 
> _"Who are you?" he asked, though part of him already knew. Part of him had always known._ 
> 
> _The figure had no face he could describe later. Only the certainty that he had seen one. That it had smiled._


> _"I am the question you haven't asked yet. The answer you keep forgetting. The first step on a road that doesn't end."_ 
> 
> _"I've been many things: guide, emperor, architect, hero, villain, innovator . I've worn names like you wear that title, [[Ashen Warrior]], poorly fitted but necessary."_


> _"They called me {...}, once. Before the [[hymn]]. Before the sundering. Before I became the space between what was and what must be."_
> 
> _The warrior's hand went to the glass pendant beneath his armor. The symbol he'd taken as his own without knowing why._


> _"You're the [[First Martyr]]."_ 
> 
> _"No, I am [[the First Question]]," the figure corrected gently._ 
> _"This is what I became when I chose to ask it: {scrubbed}"_
> 
> > _Around them, the chamber hummed. Stone older than memory. Darkness that had weight._
> 
> _"You came seeking power to save your house. But power isn't what holds the world. Questions do."_ 
> _"Questions like: What am I willing to become? What am I willing to lose? What truth can I carry that might break me?"_

> *The warrior - young still, not yet scarred by the choices that would define him, straightened.* 
> *"I'll do whatever it takes. My house falls. My family starves. I won't let them fade into nothing."* 
> 
> *"Good," the figure said, and there was both pride and sorrow in the word.*
> 
> *"That certainty will carry you far. It will cost you everything, but it will carry you."*
> *"Just remember: certainty is not the same as rightness. The surest path is often the cruelest one."* 


>  *"And when you stand at the threshold between saving those you love and becoming something they wouldn't recognize -"
>  "When they look at you with fear instead of love"
>  "When the [[hourglass]] runs empty and you realize you spent grains you didn't have the right to spend -" 
>  
>  "Remember this moment. Remember that I told you: the most important question isn't **'what can I do'** but **'what should I do.'**"
>  
>  The warrior felt cold. "You're saying I'll fail." 
>  
>  "No," the figure said. "I'm saying you'll succeed in ways you can't imagine and fail in ways you can't forgive."
>  "I'm saying you'll save your house and lose your home. You'll preserve your line and forget what made it worth preserving."*


> *"I'm saying the path you're about to walk is the only path, and it's the wrong one, and you'll walk it anyway because you're brave enough to be a fool."
> 
> Silence stretched between them like a blade. 
> 
> "Why tell me this?" the warrior finally asked. "If I'll do it anyway, why warn me?"*


> _The figure began to fade, light bleeding through where flesh should be._ 
> _"Because cycles and cycles ago, someone told me the same thing. And I didn't listen either."_ 
> _"Because the world will need to understand where you went wrong to find her own way right._ 
> _"Because the question matters even when the answer doesn't change."_


> *"And because someone has to remember that the [[hourglass]] was a symbol of time spent wisely before it became a symbol of time  ||the wind whistles, words are spoken yet nothing is heard||."*
> *The chamber returned to simple darkness. In its simple black stone.*
> *The warrior stood alone, ash still smoking on his cloak, the [[hourglass]] pendant cold against his chest.* 
> *He had come seeking power. He had found a warning.* 
> *He would ignore it, of course. He was young. His family was dying. He would do whatever it took.* 


> That was the First Question's gift and curse: you always got the answer you needed, never the one you wanted. 
> And you always, always paid for it later.


> *Years later, when the man stood in his grand hall, ready to begin his orchestrated symphony, there was an attempt to kill him, and it all unraveled. He would remember that conversation.*
> 
> *He would remember the figure's words about paths and costs and certainty.* 
> 
> *He would understand, finally, what the First Question had been asking.* 
> 
> *But by then, of course, it would be far too late to choose a different answer.* 
> 
> *The [[hourglass]] doesn't run backward.*
> **It only runs out.** 
> 
> **~**


> **The greatest folly is not finding the wrong answer, rather it is spending a lifetime solving a question that you never once stopped to understand.**
