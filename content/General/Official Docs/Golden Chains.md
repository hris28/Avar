
__Song 2: Golden Chains__
I left the thrashing (thrushing? rushing?) [[field]]s of dawn. [[Hourglass]] tight (tide?) against my chest. Each grain a bargain waiting, every split row just a test. 
I learned to bend the hidden dots, coax the numbers into line. Not to serve a higher [[mercy]], only to make that [[mercy]] [[mine]].
Measure the dawn. Measure the night.
[[Truth]] is a ledger dressed in light (lie?).
If the sun turns red, I’ll pay the fine.
Hourglass turning—thread the line.
She sang beneath the lantern tree. Silver voice in summer rain. I set my figures at her feet. Let her laughter stain the page. Dreamed of futures candlelit. But futures change when [[fracture]]s bleed. The [[field]] won’t spare what can be lost to feed a greater need.
[[Fog]] grew teeth beyond the hills, mouths that (they?) feed on memory clean. 
A broken [[shard]] of [[Honor]] wept and carved a hunger through the seams. So I forged a cage of probabilities. Lock the creature in its place. 
Let the world keep breathing. Let it never see my face.
Hourglass thinning, shadows stretch. The child walks roads I cannot tread. Ledgerings a darker script, so soft hearts will forget.
Mercy. [[Mercy]]’s just a line the fearful draw in sand. Better a [[wound]] that heals the world, than comfort without plan.
Measure the dawn. Measure the night.
I count the living. I spend the light.
When the last pact sunders, remember [[mine]]. 
Cost was way (?)
The fault is fine.
If the song of the world must end, I’ll turn the glass and start again.
Ends are means when the line is thin. 
Quiet [[eye]] (?) hourglass spin.
