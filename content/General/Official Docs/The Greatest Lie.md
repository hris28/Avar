
Life is not a riddle to be solved, it is a journey to be experienced.

> This is the greatest lie.

— The [[Grand Scholar]] {name deliberately excised from all records}, Date of Origin: 512 

_{Additional note, found scratched beneath the original inscription in a different hand}_ 

They told us to walk the path. To trust the journey. To find meaning in the movement itself rather than the destination. 
They did not mention that some paths are mazes designed by those who learned the exit and sealed it behind them. 
They did not mention that "experience" is what we call ignorance when we want it to sound noble. 
Life is absolutely a riddle. The answer is written in foundations you are not permitted to see, in names you are not allowed to speak, in [[truth]]s that unmake you if you understand them fully. 
The journey exists to keep you from asking why the destination was chosen for you.
Walk if you must. Experience if you can. But know that someone, somewhere, solved this riddle long ago. 
And they decided you were better off not knowing the answer. 

— Unknown, Estimated Date of Origin: 524
