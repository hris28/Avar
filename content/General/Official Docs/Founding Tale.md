
> They found the [[Second Martyr]] at the edge of the first sanctuary, standing before those who would return to the surface. In one hand, a blade still wet. In the other, something wrapped in cloth, something he had carried down from the dying world above, through fire and forgetting, when the sky broke and the stars turned away.
> 
> "Why?" they asked him, the survivors who had loved him, who had followed him into the dark. 
> 
> "Why do you guard it from us? Why cut away our hope when you were the one who saved it?"
> He had been a paragon once. They called him the [[Evening Star]] in those days, a [[Stellarborn]] whose light never dimmed, who had stood in leading councils and walked alongside immortals, the one who led them all into the caves, who gave everything so that humanity might have one more dawn. The Evening Star had walked further than any of them. Bled more. Loved more. He had carried [[truth]] itself through the cataclysm so that humanity might remember what it was.
> 
> And now he stood between them and that very truth, and there was such sorrow in his eyes.
> 
> He looked at what he held. He looked at them, truly looked, the way he had always looked at people, as if each one mattered more than empires. He looked at the children huddled behind their parents, too young to remember what stars looked like.
> 
> "Because I remember," he said, and his voice was gentle, the way it had always been. "I walked beside *{hissing winds}* when the sky broke. I heard the final words before the [[hymn]] took *{hissing winds}* beyond the [[veil]]. I know what they saw. What they understood. And I know why They chose to lead us down rather than let us claw our way back up." 
> 
> His voice wavered, just once, with something that might have been love or grief or both.
> 
> "He asked me to do this. Not with words. Not with commands. But I knew Him. I know Him still, in whatever space He occupies now. And this is what He would have asked: that we survive. That we learn. That we carry forward not the mistakes, but the love that made them worth making."
> 
> He sealed [[truth]] with his own blood and his own words, speaking the first prayer of the new doctrine, not to a distant lord, but to the memory of his friend, to the faith that all their sacrifice would mean something. That humanity could endure, and perhaps, one day, be gentle again.
> 
> "I carried this so that one day, when we are ready, when we are wise, someone might open it and understand. But that day is not today. And I will stand here until that day comes, or until I fall ensuring it might."
> 
> Those who loved him wept. Those who hated him, who saw in him only the ghost of the glory he once embodied, now turned to ash and duty, raised their blades. He did not flee. He did not fight. He had been a warrior without equal in the world above. He could have cut them all down.
> 
> Instead, he knelt before the vault he had [[seal]]ed and spoke his final prayer aloud, so that all might hear. He prayed for their survival. He prayed for their children. He prayed that they would forget what must be forgotten, but never forget how to be kind to one another in the dark.
> 
> He prayed that his friend, wherever He was now, sundered and sustained by humanity's [[hymn]], would forgive him for what came next. And he prayed that they would forgive each other, in time.
> 
> When the first blade fell, he was still praying.
> When the last blade fell, those who struck him were weeping too.
> His prayer became the foundation of everything that followed.
> **"We carry the burden so humanity need not remember the weight."**
> 
> *The [[Inquisition]] was born from his [[blood]] and his faith and his love. We do not carry his name. It was the first thing we buried, because he asked us to. We carry his work. We guard what he saved, and we guard humanity from it, until the day, if such a day ever comes, when we are worthy of the gentleness he showed us, even at the end.*

~ The [[Founding Tale]]
