

> They called me [[Law]] when laws had no names. They called me [[Oath]] when oaths were only a breath of the night. Later they called me nothing, and I learned how quiet a title can be. 
> 
> I have walked the long stair between skies. I have stood on shores that were not seas and watched eternities fold like cloaks. Do not mistake the word. Eternity is not a span. It is a habit the world refuses to break. I broke it once. Perhaps I stood where she knew I would stand, and the rhythm broke as it must. She did not tell me. She looked once, the way a horizon looks at a storm it has already chosen. If she still kept the path, she would be here. I loved the world as she loved it, which is to say I loved it enough to cut it free. The quiet that followed took her place beneath my ribs and has not lifted. One day you will stand where I am standing now. You will understand, and understanding will not spare you. 
> 
> Listen. The first [[veil]] of the world did not rise like a mountain. It was woven, a [[mercy]] pulled across a [[wound]] so the story could keep breathing. When it thinned and forgetting learned to sing, I was no maker, only a keeper. So was bound a second skin over the world, a bandage. The last weapon in a failing hand. An unbreakable shield held against a darkness that would not bargain. One final blade shaped in defiance of the ending that waited. 
> 
> Know this. Law is not a [[wall]]. Law is a choice carried past comfort, a line held after it cuts. I carried it first. I vanished first. If you must step through the [[seam]], go knowing what you bear. Go knowing what was lost and what will be lost. Go knowing why. 
>

> 
> Let this be my final litany. Let this be my final lament. Let this be my requiem.
