
They called him the [[Fifth Martyr]], though he was the first to bear such a title while still drawing breath. 

[[Jorell]] {the surname has been deliberately excised from all records} was born with [[eye]]s the color of spilled rubies, a trait that marked the royal bloodline since before the descent. The crimson gaze that could see through deception, through shadow, through the thin places where reality wore threadbare. A gift. A curse. A responsibility carried in the color of his sight. 

When humanity huddled in the caves of [[Avar]], scattered and afraid, it was Jorell who saw what others could not: the pattern of survival, the shape of what must be built, the location where the capital must stand. 

"Here," he said, standing in a cavern where five tunnels met, where the stone hummed with old power, where something beneath listened without malice. "Here is where we anchor ourselves. Not because it is safe. Because it is right."

He was twenty-three when he established the seat of the capital. Twenty-four when he realized what it would cost him. 

**From His Private Journal** 
_Script degraded but legible, ink mixed with something that tests as partially biological_ 

**Year 2, Third Cycle** 
The others do not understand why I insist on this location. They feel the weight here, the wrongness, and they want to build elsewhere. Somewhere lighter. Somewhere that does not press against the chest like a hand holding you underwater. 

But I see what they cannot. This is the thinnest point. This is where the seal must be reinforced by presence, by civilization, by the weight of living humanity standing watch whether they know it or not.

I will build the capital here. And I will remain here. And I will watch.

**Year 3, Eightieth Cycle** 
I do not sleep well anymore. When I close my eyes, I see through the stone. See the thing beneath, vast and patient, coiled around itself in geometries that make no sense to waking minds. It does not threaten. It does not move. It simply is, and the being of it presses against the [[seal]] like water against a dam. 

The gift of sight, they said. The bloodline's blessing, they said. 

They did not mention that some truths carve themselves into you. That seeing becomes a form of touching. That observation is a kind of contact. 

**Year 7, First Cycle** 
I felt it today. The bond forming. 

I am becoming part of the watching. My sight, the sight that pierces barriers, is creating a connection I cannot sever. Every time I look down, every time I check the [[seal]], every time I verify that it holds, I tie myself more thoroughly to this place.

To this purpose. 

I will not live a long life. I understand that now. But the capital will stand. The watching will continue. And when I finally close my eyes for the last time, perhaps they will remain open in some way that matters. 

**Year 12, Second Cycle** 
They want to name me king. I have refused four times. A king can leave. A king can delegate. A king can choose when his duty ends. 

I am not a king. 

I am a [[Martyr]]. The fifth, they will call me, after the four who came before and gave themselves to other seals, other watchings, other necessary endings. 

I accept the title. I reject the crown. 

Let the families rule. I have a different throne to sit upon. One carved from obligation and sight and the terrible gift of vision that will not look away.

**Year 13, Three-Hundredth Cycle**
I have made a decision. My family name will be stricken from all records. No dynasty will claim descent from me. No house will use my [[blood]] as justification for power. 

The watching is not hereditary. It is chosen. Or rather, it chooses. 

Let my name be [[Jorell]], and nothing more. Let my legacy be the capital that stands, not a lineage that grasps for thrones they did not earn. 

The priests resist. The [[chronicle]]rs protest. I do not care. 
Strike it. Burn it. Erase it from history. I will not be the founder of a dynasty.

**Year 19, Five Hundredth and Thirty Eighth Cycle** 
I am forgetting what it feels like to not see. My gaze never rests now, even when I sleep. Even in dreams, I am watching. The thing beneath shifts in rhythms I have learned to read like a heartbeat. When it stirs, I feel it before the instruments do. When it settles, I know we have another season of safety. 

This is not life anymore. This is vigil given form. 

But the capital stands. Humanity stands here, unaware of what I see, of what I hold at bay through nothing more than constant observation and the weight of attention that will not break

**Year 24, Five Hundredth and Thirty Ninth Cycle** 
I am told I no longer eat. That I no longer speak except to warn of instabilities. That I sit in the deepest chamber of the capital, staring at stone that I see through as easily as [[glass]]. 

They bring scholars to study me. They bring priests to pray over me. They do not understand that I am not sick. I am not failing. 
I am completing. 

The bloodline's sight was always meant for this. Curse of the royal reds as they say. 

Sleep feels like an inevitable farewell now. Each time I drift toward it, I wonder if this is the moment I stop being a man and become only the watching. Waking feels like the final turning of a page, the last words of a story whose ending was written the moment I chose this location and said "here." 

I do not regret it. 

But I do mourn what I was, before I became what I am. 

I feel I have but one last note to impart. 
It will be noted tomorrow.
