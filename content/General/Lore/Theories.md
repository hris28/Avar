### Pre-Cataclysm
- Humanity originally lived above ground.
- Society is governed by astronomy. The stars are treated as absolute truth.

> *Some are born with the night in their blood: Stellarborn, marked by a talent that does not come from tutors or kings. A very few climb further and do not come back the same.*

- Magic was tied to cosmic heritage, not divine intervention.

> *Lines that should be steady smear and slip. A soundless wind moves through observatories and waystations at once, and instruments that have never faltered begin to lie. Messages arrive half-remembered. Sailors see two horizons.*

- Overreaching magical/astronomical experimentation woke something old. It causes instrumentation to become meaningless and memory/perception to corrupt and contaminate.

> *The astronomers saw it first in calculations that suddenly refused to align. The stars were right, but the answer was wrong.*

- This implies that what failed first wasn't astronomy. It was cognition itself--the link between truth and interpretation.
- Since our attempts to derive truth are now corrupted, any attempt to interpret will only hasten collapse. Trying to interpret a truth you can no longer correctly process creates a false reality.
- This explains why "lines smear and slip" when observed. They’re stable until we look. Our perception destabilizes them.
- So it's like a Schrodinger's Cat situation. The danger isn't the stars, but rather the act of forcing a coherent answer from them as cognition breaks.
- *to those who know*, I wonder if this is somewhat akin to how in Stormlight, gambling is seen as bad because it relies on "predicting the future" and trying to predict the future is forbidden because it can be influenced? In this world, the stars are literally used to predict the future in a sense and in various ways.

> *You fought them with everything you had. Velderra's greatest warriors, her brightest Stellarborn, her most devoted astronomers calculating strike patterns by planetary motion, all thrown against the incursion.*

- I'm unsure if the [[Urd]] are a physical race, but the way they're spoken in other places on the preamble, it's as if the Urd aren't physical invaders but rather parasitic forces that "live inside" cognition and memory. In the realm of thought and interpretation.

> *They came through a tear you did not mean to make: scholars seeking new worlds, new knowledge, new power, reaching through the veil until something reached back.*

- So perhaps the act of attempting to understand the cosmos causes the cosmos to react back to prevent that understanding? through the Urd?

> *The Urd were not the invasion. They were the symptom.*

- Humanity caused this through its attempt to understand. The true threat is the wound inside collective consciousness.
- It is implied that certainty, belief, and familiar cognitive structures (“prayers,” “lullabies,” patterns) are where The Urd take root. And that the attempt to understand--the "reaching"--is what caused all this

> *You are not abandoning anyone. You are spending yourselves...Deliver it to those who will forget why they needed it.*

- Survival strategy is delay, not victory.
- Implies that even future survivors (like Vo’dyn leaders) would lose the context, but the ledger would carry emotion and core truths even if interpretation fails.

> *Inside are names. Streets. Truths that the coming darkness will try to erase. A list of worlds...dreams...places...of humanity. A story of what must not be forgotten, even when forgetting becomes the world's only language.*

- The ledger doesn't contain theories, predictions, calculations, or attempts to force understanding. It is raw truth without structured knowledge. Something that feels, not something that explains.
