> Are the existence of Martyr's Day and Remembrance Day common knowledge?

Within the Empire of Vo'dyn, yes. They are known as other names in differing empires cultures.

> Are they considered holidays similar to how we do?

Holidays in this world are not too comparable to our world.

> Is there any historic information about these events that would have been taught as commonly known?

Yes. "[[Him]]" led Vo'dyn in the caves on [[Remembrance Day]] and the following Martyr's Day gave his life for the empire. In addition, the [[Great Martyred Lady]] fell on another Martyr's Day.

> There are multiple Martyrs Days?

It falls on the same day of the calendar (dubious dating quality due to calendar practices). 
There are separate more specific days for nicher cases that fall under differing names.

- [[Remembrance Day]] would be cycle 0 (for the day of Remembrance is the 'start' of all that mattered). Martyr's Day occurred on cycle -1 (but this was the before times...where the world was Martyred and chaos swept it aside...thus not fully canonical in the religious dating system)...followed by Martyr's Day a year later.
- "Him" sacrificed his spirit on the first remembrance day (making him the [[First Martyr]])...giving it so that the rest of the Vo'dyn may live. While stories/messages differ onto how- all agree that it was just as the clock struck the final hour.
- The Great Martyred Lady died some ~426 years later after this event also on Martyr's day. Records of this are incomplete due to ecclestical tampering along with the burning of so many archives/destruction. Vo'dyn was experiencing some profound change...something sweeping the empire and The Great Martyred Lady rode out using her magical powers- her curse for the sake of Vo'dyn...ultimately the powers claimed her life and those around her (but in her and their sacrifice) Vo'dyn held. It is rumored she was also the founder of the [[Luminary council]].
