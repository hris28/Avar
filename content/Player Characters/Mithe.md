Mithe Demacia Khol was born of Lux Demacia, a high elf far from home, and Midas Khol, a man whose name was already heavy in the First Circle before she ever drew breath.

She lived in the center of the Capitol.

Not as a daughter.  
Not as a noble.  
But as something more dangerous: a secret.

She and her mother were housed within a controlled wing of the central estate in the First Circle — close enough to power to feel its heat, far enough to never be acknowledged by it. Officially, she was nothing. Unofficially, she was monitored, guarded, and educated.

	She and Lux were housed **within a controlled wing**, likely classified as:
	
	- A charitable annex
	    
	- A quiet patronage arrangement
	    
	- A “foreign guest” situation
	    
	- Or attached to someone else’s household entirely
	    
	
	On paper, Midas had nothing to do with them.
	
	In practice?
	
	He funded the arrangement through intermediaries.
	
	He did not educate you personally.
	
	He did not present you.
	
	He did not risk proximity.
	
	But he ensured:
	
	- You were fed
	    
	- You were tutored
	    
	- You were monitored
	    
	- You were contained
	    
	
	Not out of warmth.
	
	Out of risk management.
	
	You were not a daughter.
	
	You were a loose end.

Midas did not parade her.  
But he did not discard her either.

He attempted to remove visible traces of himself from her — most notably her eye. When arcane alteration failed and left the whites of her eyes permanently blackened, harsher options were considered. Lux intervened. A compromise was made.

The eye would remain.  
The child would remain.  
But the story would change.

From ages 0-9, Mithe grew up wandering the edges of marble halls. She remembers watching important figures enter and leave. Quiet meetings behind silver doors. The sound of armored boots and whispered arguments. A man in polished armor who once gave her a necklace and told her it kept nightmares away.

---
Then something shifted.

Scandal grows in silence before it breaks.

Around age nine, Mithe and Lux were quietly removed from the central estate. Not violently — but definitively. They were relocated to the outer First Circle and given the name Davarre.

House Davarre served as both shield and exile.  
The official narrative changed.  
Records were amended.  
The ward became the daughter of Alren Davarre.

From that point forward, Mithe no longer lived at the center of power.

But she had already memorized its layout.

In House Davarre, Mithe lived in a strange duality.

She was not First Circle elite anymore — but she was not common either.

She attended balls occasionally — sometimes as a minor noble attendee, sometimes assisting at more exclusive gatherings. She learned to move between status tiers fluidly. She watched how alliances were formed. She listened more than she spoke.

She made friends among staff — especially servants. They were less guarded. They saw her more clearly than nobles did.

And she learned how to slip out.

This is where your sneakiness fits beautifully.

Because she had grown up in the Capitol center, she understood guard rotations, servant entrances, and the blind spots of authority.

Lux’s illness deepened during their time under Davarre.

Midas did provide medical care — but distant, measured care. Not the desperation of a father. The containment of a noble managing a liability.

Mithe studied medicine obsessively. Healing magic. Herbcraft. Disease theory. She pleaded with specialists. She burned through favors she didn’t truly have.

At 16, she confronted Midas directly.

Not as a ward.  
Not as a Davarre daughter.  
But as his child.

And he refused further intervention.

Not cruelly.  
Coldly.

Care would not escalate.  
Resources would not expand.  
And if she continued pressing, the arrangement itself would be reconsidered.

Lux fell into a coma.

Mithe left briefly in desperation to seek unconventional aid.

When she returned, Lux was dead.

Her attempt on Midas’ life was not impulsive rage.

It was betrayal.

She had grown up believing he was restrained but protective.

That illusion shattered.

Her attack failed.

She was imprisoned.

This prison period is where her identity fractures. She is no longer ward, no longer Davarre daughter, no longer secret Kholin. She becomes something else.

Her brief escape at 17 and descent into survival sex work isn’t about scandal — it’s about dislocation. She no longer belongs to any structure. She tries to build one. It nearly kills her.

By 18 she is recaptured.

Mithe spent her early childhood inside the capital under the quiet protection of Alren Davarre, raised as his ward and publicly regarded as his daughter. Educated, dressed well, and properly briefed — but never fully claimed — she grew up in the margins of nobility. Ignored enough to be invisible, she began slipping beyond the estate walls, discovering the city’s underbelly and forging connections far from courtly eyes. There, she learned to blend, to listen, and to speak in hands as well as words.

When exile came, it did not strip her of privilege — it stripped her of the illusion that she ever truly had it.
