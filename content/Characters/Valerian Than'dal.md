A boy under the wing and tutelage of [[Midas]].


---
He is not a retainer. Not merely a bodyguard.
He is a ward. A weapon.

And Mithe’s breath hitches.

She doesn’t know the man.
But she knows that armor.

DC :
She’d seen it once through the tall gallery glass of the Observatory of Names, years before her exile—her father, Midas, striding across the marble floor toward a closed council.

Midas hadn’t looked her way. He never did.

But beside him walked a boy. Younger. Clean-faced. Silent.
Not yet cloaked in status, but held close. As if important. As if fragile.

Her mother had noticed, too.

“That one clings to your father like a second shadow,” she whispered.
“You watch. He’ll either die for him, or because of him.”

DC
Then—something sharper.
Not from the gallery. From the corridor behind the council chamber.

She’d gotten lost. Wandering where she shouldn’t. A forgotten girl, in a forgotten dress, in a place that never welcomed her.

He had found her.
Not yet a knight. Not yet sealed in steel.

He’d simply offered her a way back.

“The silver doors lead to the marble stairs. That’s where the soft-sound guards stop watching.”

No title. No scorn. Just simple words, and a glance that felt almost like acknowledgment.
The only kindness she remembered from that entire week.

DC :
That night, through a cracked study door, her father had spoken softly—unusually so.

“The boy swore too soon,” Midas said, his voice wrapped in worry and wine.
“I should have stopped him.”

Her mother’s voice came sharp, scared:

“Then why let him take the vow?”

And her father, cold again, but not cruel:

“Because if he hadn’t, he would’ve broken instead.”
